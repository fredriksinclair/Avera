A few examples of what this looks like in practice.

Engagements are kept confidential. These are described in general terms with permission.

Ownership had drifted. Meetings were covering the same ground.

Context

A forty-person leadership team across three functions had lost clarity on who owned what. Decisions were slow. The same questions surfaced in multiple meetings each week because no one was confident who could resolve them.

What we did

We spent three weeks listening — separately to team leads and to the people doing the work. We mapped how decisions and information actually moved, identified where ownership had become ambiguous, and produced a plain account of what we found. From there, we simplified one core operating process and clarified decision authority for the recurring situations that were causing the most friction.

Outcome

No new tools were introduced. Meetings shortened. The repeated questions stopped. Within six weeks, team leads reported spending significantly less time in coordination that had previously produced no resolution.

Onboarding existed on paper. In practice, every manager ran it differently.

Context

A growing professional services firm was onboarding new staff slowly and inconsistently. The formal process existed, but each manager interpreted it differently. New hires spent their first weeks unclear on what was expected and who to ask. Time to useful contribution was longer than it needed to be.

What we did

We interviewed managers and recent hires separately, then compared what each group said about the same process. The gaps were significant. We identified what actually helped new people become useful quickly, removed steps that served process rather than the person, and rebuilt the sequence around what the work actually required in the first thirty days.

Outcome

Onboarding time dropped by roughly a third. New hires reported feeling ready to contribute sooner. Managers stopped filling in the gaps informally — the process was consistent enough that they no longer had to.
