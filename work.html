Work

A few examples of what this looks like in practice.

Engagements are kept confidential. These are described in general terms with permission.

Examples

A leadership team was overseeing more than a dozen active initiatives.

Most were moving, but slowly.

Attention shifted week to week. What was important depended on what was most visible.

We looked at how leadership time was actually spent across those initiatives.

It didn’t match the stated priorities.

They reduced the number of active initiatives and clarified which ones required direct involvement.

Some work was paused. Some moved forward without them.

Progress became less evenly distributed, but more deliberate.

— Recent engagement

A company had defined an operating model during a previous phase of growth.

Roles, responsibilities, and decision points were documented.

In practice, people worked around it.

Decisions escalated unpredictably. Some areas moved quickly, others stalled.

We spent time with teams to see how decisions were actually being made.

They adjusted the model to match reality and removed parts that weren’t being used.

Fewer exceptions were needed afterward.

Work followed a more predictable path.

— Recent engagement

A leadership team set clear quarterly priorities.

But they didn’t consistently translate into team-level work.

Some areas over-indexed on them. Others ignored them entirely.

We looked at how priorities were being turned into plans.

There was no clear link between the two.

They introduced a simple layer to connect priorities to execution.

Over time, the work began to reflect the intent.
